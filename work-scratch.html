Characterizing the Visibility of Scratches

Role: Data Compilation, Data Analysis

During a 3-month internship in Essilor, I studied the relationship between the topography of scratches (such as depth and width) on spectacle lenses and the visibility of the scratches after a coating is applied on the lens. I was introduced to data science and learnt VBA for Excel to extract and interpret large amounts of data. I also wrote a report on the work I did. I will be an author on the peer-reviewed scientific paper the team will publish.

Project Motivation

When a prescription lens is produced, cosmetic scratches can form on the surface of the lens. The lens is given a coating for anti-scratch and anti-reflection properties. The coating can usually cover up the scratches that are already on the lens. However, if any scratch is visible after coating, the lens is rejected and produced again. Technical workers inspect scratches by eye but this requires skill and experience.

Performing the Experiments

I worked with Stephane who is a PhD student. Stephane had already started on the project before I started my internship. He produced a number of lenses and used an indentation machine to make precise scratches on the lenses. He then collected the surface topography measurements of the scratches. When I started my internship, I took up where Stephane left off. I completed the experiments by coating the lenses. I also collected more data such as taking photos of the lenses before and after the coating.


Extracting and Analysing the Data

My main contribution was in the data extraction and data analysis. I learnt how to use VBA to extract the relevant surface topography data. I also developed an objective metric to quantify the visiblity of scratches using an image analysis software ImageJ. Putting the surface topography and visiblity data into a statistical analysis software, I managed to get a preliminary result. At a very high statistical significance, the width of a scratch would have to be more than 19.6 micrometers for the scratch to be visible. Other parameters such as the depth of scratch did not affect the visiblity of the scratch as much.
