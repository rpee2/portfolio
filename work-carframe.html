Materials Selection for Car Frame

Role: Research, Design Thinking, Building

Overview

In my materials selection class, we were exposed to real-world materials knowledge. It provided an introductory link between the theory of materials science to the application of materials. I used Ashby plots in CES Edupack for a class project to select a material to optimize the function of a product.

Class

When designing a product, a material is selected based on a range of properties such that it satisfies the intended purpose of the product. In my class, we familiarized ourselves with the CES Edupack, which is a software containing a database of 4000 commonly used materials and their properties. The goal is to narrow down early in the design process a list of materials for the product using the database. I did a case study for the class on the material selection for mass-produced car frames.

Process

I worked in a team of four. I was involved in researching the function of a car frame. I learnt that the function of a car frame is to 1) provide good ride and handling of the car, 2) increase the fuel efficiency of the car and 3) provide safety to the occupants in the car. I identified the properties that relate to the function of a car frame. One example is that a car frame with lower density will decrease the car weight which increases the fuel efficiency of the car. Finally, I created a chart that visualized the material selection process my team would attempt.


My teammates were involved in finding the relevant properties of current materials used in car frames as benchmarks. They created the Ashby plots to narrow down the material. We identified 30 materials (?) having a combination of properties that satisfied the function of the car frame. AISI 5160 low alloy steel was selected in the end because it had the highest fracture toughness out of the other suitable materials.
